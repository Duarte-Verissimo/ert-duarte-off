# Requirements Engineering Home

<h2 align="center"><mark style="color:blue;"><strong>Requirements Engineering &#x26; Testing — Lab Classes</strong></mark></h2>

Welcome to the **lab (practical)** component of this course.\
Throughout the semester you will work on a **case study** (or an approved 1st-semester project) and build an incremental GitHub dossier covering:

- **Requirements Engineering**: elicitation, structured requirements, REM, user stories, acceptance criteria, DoD, use cases, traceability
- **Testing**: strategy, manual test cases, automation with **PyTest/JUnit**, and **BDD/Gherkin** with executable scenarios

> Final goal: demonstrate a complete, traceable chain\
> **Requirement → Acceptance Criteria → BDD Scenario/Test Case → Running Test**

### <mark style="color:blue;">How we work in labs</mark>

- Teams of **3–4 students** with rotating roles (facilitator, scribe, reviewer, tester)
- Sessions follow a **“micro-theory + hands-on”** format
- Incremental deliverables, always focusing on:
  - clear, testable requirements
  - objective acceptance criteria
  - traceability (REQ ↔ US ↔ UC ↔ TC/BDD)
  - running tests (PyTest/JUnit and BDD)

#### <mark style="color:blue;">**“Using the AMS Case Study”**</mark>

- You may use the AMS GitBook to understand the domain and workflows.
- You must not copy user stories, requirements, or acceptance criteria text from the case study.
- Your team will receive a variant assignment (persona/constraints/workflow). Your deliverables must reflect your variant.
- Submissions that match the case study wording will be rejected for that item.
